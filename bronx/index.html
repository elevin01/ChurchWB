Reading List

Books about the Bronx.

The Bronx has a rich, complex story — told through jazz clubs and hip hop parks, fires and rebuilding, immigrants and lifelong families. These books help tell that story.

  • Paradise Bronx: The Life and Times of New York's Greatest Borough Ian Frazier — Farrar, Straus and Giroux
  • Before the Fires: An Oral History of African American Life in the Bronx from the 1930s to the 1960s Mark Naison & Bob Gumbs — Fordham University Press
  • The Bronx Nobody Knows: An Urban Walking Guide William B. Helmreich — Princeton University Press
  • Ladies and Gentlemen, the Bronx Is Burning: 1977, Baseball, Politics, and the Battle for the Soul of a City Jonathan Mahler — Picador
  • Just Kids from the Bronx: Telling It the Way It Was: An Oral History Arlene Alda — Henry Holt
  • Yankees by the Numbers: A Complete Team History of the Bronx Bombers by Uniform Number Bill Gutman — Skyhorse Publishing
  • Born in the Bronx: A Visual Record of the Early Days of Hip Hop Joe Conzo — Rizzoli
  • The Birth of the Bronx, 1609–1900 Lloyd Ultan — Bronx County Historical Society
  • The City of Hip-Hop: New York City, The Bronx, and a Peace Meeting Rasul A. Mowatt & Rob Swift — Routledge
  • Bronx Accent: A Literary and Pictorial History of the Borough Lloyd Ultan — Rutgers University Press
  • Bibliotherapy in the Bronx Emely Rumble — Simon & Schuster

Places to Visit

The Bronx is worth exploring.

Parks, gardens, history, food, and culture — all within one borough. Here are some of our favorite places to visit near us.

  • Bronx Zoo One of the largest urban zoos in the world
  • Arthur Avenue — Little Italy of the Bronx The real Little Italy, with old-world markets, bakeries, and restaurants
  • New York Botanical Garden 250 acres of living collections and landscape in the heart of the Bronx
  • Van Cortlandt Park NYC's third-largest park — trails, forests, and open fields
  • Wave Hill Public Gardens Breathtaking gardens overlooking the Hudson River and Palisades
  • City Island A small New England-style fishing village tucked into the Bronx
  • Pelham Bay Park NYC's largest park, with beaches, trails, and salt marshes
  • The Hip Hop Museum Coming to Bronx Point — honoring the birthplace of hip hop
  • Edgar Allan Poe Cottage The last home of Poe, now a historic landmark in the Bronx
  • Yankee Stadium Home of the New York Yankees in the South Bronx
